Plating Technique

It is very important that you use the correct plating technique to inoculate your growth media. Using an improper technique may lead to an undesirable distribution of bacterial cells.

In some situations you may wish to inoculate your media with an even distribution of bacteria to create a 'lawn'. This is achieved through the spread plate technique.

Method

  1. Set the pipette volume
    1. Set the desired volume and place the correct sized tip on the pipette.
    2. Do not let the tip come into contact with any surface as it will become contaminated.
  2. Pipette culture onto plate
    1. Open the culture bottle and hold the lid in the same hand. Hold the pipette in the other hand, draw up a measured aliquot of the culture and then close the lid.
    2. Carefully dispense a measured aliquot of your culture onto the surface of an agar plate whilst holding the lid over the agar plate.
  3. Spread culture around plate
    1. Take a sterile spreader and spread the aliquot evenly around the whole surface.
    2. Once the spreader has been discarded the plate will be ready to be incubated with the agar side up.

Equipment

  • Pipette
  • Culture
  • Agar Plate
  • Spreader
